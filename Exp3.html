The cat is a small, graceful domestic mammal that has been a companion to humans for thousands of years. Known scientifically as Felis catus, they are famous for their soft fur, sharp retractable claws, and ability to see clearly in very dim light. Cats are highly independent yet affectionate, often communicating through gentle meows, purrs, and trills. As obligate carnivores, they have a natural instinct for hunting small pests like mice, which historically made them valuable to farmers and homeowners. Today, they are one of the most popular pets worldwide, loved for their playful antics, clean habits, and the calming presence they bring to a home.

Computer Science Engineering (CSE) is an academic program that integrates the fields of computer engineering and computer science to focus on the design, development, and maintenance of both software and hardware systems. It is one of the most popular and rapidly evolving engineering disciplines, driven by the increasing global demand for innovation in Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), cybersecurity, and cloud computing. Throughout a 4-year degree, students study foundational subjects such as programming languages (Java, Python, C++), algorithms, data structures, operating systems, and computer architecture. Graduates of this field are equipped with strong analytical and problem-solving skills, making them highly sought after for roles as software developers, system analysts, and database administrators across various industries, including technology, finance, healthcare, and manufacturing. Ultimately, CSE is essential for shaping the modern digital world, providing the expertise to build advanced technologies like robotics, IoT, and next-generation transportation systems.
